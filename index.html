about me

I’m Robin McCormick, a nonbinary, Montreal-based experimental game designer. My personal journey has inspired me to develop a unique narrative style that can be characterized as contemplative, immersive, and atmospheric. I consider creation to be deeply personal and yet relatable: from the subject matter to the medium, game designers' choices are all intentional and a reﬂection of their personality. I reﬂect deeply during the design and authorship process about the ways to craft my message and create an emotionally engaging and narratively impactful game. The game engine's limitations, the kind of graphics, the type of writing or lack thereof: what best embodies the project? By working with a variety of mediums, I can tailor every concept to its completion.

I’ve grown quite adaptable over time, building a solid foundation in a broad range of technological areas: computer programming (including Javascript, C# in Unity, 3D modelling, interaction design, and beyond! I understand many aspects of the game-creation pipeline due to this variety, which allows for good communication and understanding in teams. I’m also quick to learn new tools and adapt to new environments, as well as take on a variety of roles. Ultimately, I’m hoping to diversify and grow even further as a designer!

a typical day at the grocery store

'a typical day at the grocery store' is a tiny vignette created in Bitsy 3D about how our typical way of life has been alienated by the ongoing pandemic. The limited space of the engine simulates claustrophobic shelves and hyper awareness of the space between our bodies. The faceless patrons move around sporadically, and a cheerful jingle playing in the background only ampliﬁes the dissonance and discomfort. Ultimately, 'a typical day at the grocery store' hopes to capture the very real shift in thought and behavior in completing once menial and nearly thoughtless tasks.

the forest

In the wake of the 2020 election that took place in the United States, political ideologies have been even more prevalent in daily discourse. Visually depicting the language that surrounds these ideologies as trees is representative of the ways people grow and branch oﬀ into varying communities and ideologies. I scraped data with a Python script on most-used words in political subreddits, created tree models in Blender with a texture created from these words, and put everything together on a webpage using the Javascript library A-Frame. The result is an interactive scene where the roots share common words used by both communities depicted, and the trees branch oﬀ into more speciﬁc, individual terms. The Forest makes a visual statement and allows its viewers to explore and come to their own conclusion.

TRANSMUSEUM

TRANSMUSEUM was a passion project curated to refute misinformation about trans people in a simple, easy to understand way. I explored the idea of using the Bitsy game engine to curate a tiny virtual museum. This led me to be able to work within the engine’s ‘architecture’, focusing on how to best visualize and represent data within a two-dimensional, digital space as opposed to a three-dimensional, real-life exhibit. This allowed for some artistic liberties as well as ‘gamic’ depictions of data, such as climbing a virtual staircase to signify the steps that trans people go through during transition. Ultimately, TRANSMUSEUM presents a few basic information visualizations to pique visitors' curiosity and hopefully incite further research.

what is this sleep which holds you now

This short vignette game is based oﬀ of a scene in the Epic of Gilgamesh, where Gilgamesh spends his time at Enkidu’s bedside as he is fatally ill. By interacting with various objects in the scene through a simple drag and drop script, the player (as Gilgamesh) takes on the role of both a bystander and caretaker for Enkidu at the end of his life. There are no points and no signals that Enkidu is getting any better as a result of their actions. The scene is displayed as still and monochromatic to emphasize this delicate and diﬃcult moment- the actions small, and with little impact on Enkidu's state, but the meaning is derived wholly from the caretaker's point of view.

a new normal

Created for the GAMERella Game Jam in 2020, this vignette is a rhythmic and eventually looping experience that aims to capture the cognitive distortion of time during the pandemic. As I used ﬂickgame’s engine to bring this project to life, the mechanics are fairly simple - clicking through certain areas will lead the player through the days, which begin to blur in confusion. By eventually falling into a loop, everything begins to look exactly the same and the days all blur together.
